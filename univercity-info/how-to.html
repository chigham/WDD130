Perhaps you are already convinced of the benefits of ditching your car for UniverCity living. Maybe you already have. Or you might want to be less dependent on your car but don't know how. Here are practical applications for how you can rely more on your feet, public transportation in your community, and smaller sustainable vehicles like bikes and scooters. These principles apply whether you live on campus or in a small or large city with relevant transportation infrastructure and services.

Where to Start

The first thing to do is consider if your community actually allows for UniverCity living transportation options. If you are in a rural home outside of town, transit may not be an option, but walking and biking can be. If you live in a tiny apartment with limited storage, a personal bike may not make sense. Physical disabilities must also be considered.

Practical Tips and Tricks

For Students

  • Find out if your campus has a shuttle that services your home
  • Does the local public transit agency offer free or discounted passes to students?
  • Take a class on civic engagement or local government and research communities for your projects

Two Feet

  • Buy the best walking shoes you can reasonably afford
  • If your destination is less than 3/4 miles, walk
  • Consider clothing other than shoes and an umbrella
  • Stay safe! Walk with someone at night if you can and invest in pepper spray or a rape whistle.

Transit and Small Vehicles

  • Download free apps like transit agency apps, Citymapper and Lime
  • Plan your trips with Google Maps and use the transit option
  • Purchase or lease a bike or eScooter
  • Make friends who use transit and share ideas with each other

Ideas for Parents

Take your kids downtown for a day trip and leave the car at home. Bring cash or purchase fare passes in advance, and bring your walking shoes. This shows the kids that UniverCity living can be fun, and you might be surprised by enjoying it yourself!

When possible, run errands with your kids on foot or on the bus. Talk about their life, bond, take notice of your surroundings. Talk to different kinds of beautiful people you meet and wish them well. Set the example of UniverCity living, even if only occasionally.

There's so much you can do! Buy your kids bikes and scooters and encourage them to use them. Discuss their favorite places to walk or bike and what parts of town they consider dangerous and why they think that is. Take them to a city meeting and talk about it. Visit a college campus. Show them a zoning map and discuss each type of zone. Get creative!
